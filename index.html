Characters in Noli Me Tangere
"Noli Me Tangere" is a seminal Filipino novel written by Jose Rizal in 1887. It tells the story of Juan Crisostomo Ibarra's return to the Philippines, where he uncovers the injustices and corruption of Spanish colonial rule, ultimately leading to a call for reform and societal change.
Juan Crisostomo Ibarra Maria Clara Captain Tiago Padre Damaso Padre Salvi


Juan Crisostomo Ibarra
Full Name: Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magsalin
Ethnicity: Spanish mestizo
Relations: Don Rafael Ibarra (father), Maria Clara (fiancée)
Description: The novel's protagonist and a Filipino who has studied in Europe. He returns to the Philippines to build a school in his hometown and to marry his fiancée, Maria Clara.


Maria Clara
Full Name: Maria Clara de los Santos
Ethnicity: Spanish mestiza
Relations: Capitan Tiago (adoptive father), Doña Pia Alba (mother), Padre Damaso (godfather; biological father), Tia Isabel (aunt), Crisostomo Ibarra (fiancée)
Description: Ibarra's fiancée, who is beautiful and modest. She is the daughter of Captain Tiago and a symbol of purity in the novel.


Captain Tiago
Full Name: Santiago de los Santos
Ethnicity: Indio
Relations: Doña Pia Alba (wife), Maria Clara (adoptive daughter)
Description: A wealthy and benevolent mestizo who is Maria Clara's father. He is a close friend of the Spanish friars and a well-connected figure in society.


Padre Damaso
Full Name: Damaso Verdolagas
Ethnicity: Spanish
Relations: Maria Clara (goddaughter; daughter)
Description: A corrupt and arrogant friar who is the parish priest in San Diego. He is one of the main antagonists in the story, often clashing with Ibarra.


Padre Salvi
Full Name: Bernardo Salvi
Ethnicity: Spanish
Relations: N/A
Description: Another friar who is more cunning and manipulative than Padre Damaso. He has a particular interest in Maria Clara and becomes entangled in the novel's conflicts.
